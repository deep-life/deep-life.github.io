Dataset

The dataset of protein sequences used for both Feedback and GAN pre-training was obtained from open-source Protein Secondary Structure Dataset from Kaggle. The whole dataset contains 95,915 samples of varying length with up to 800 amino acids in a sequence. We have used sequences with up to 75 amino acids (= 225 letters in DNA coding). This resuls in over 24,000 training samples.

To limit the number of characters GAN has to learn, protein samples in this dataset were translated to DNA sequences. This translation is one-to-one (with a few expections that are not-siginificant for this task) and thus no information is lost during translation.

Overall, we can see that dataset is unbalanced. This, however, is representative of how features are distributed in real life. The dominating features of dataset (C,H,E) are also the dominating features in nature. And the rarest features like pi-helix (I) also barely appear in the dataset. We were also curious to see if existence of any of the features heavily indicates presence of others. In other words, given that X is in the sequence, how many Y features will the sequences? As expected, C,H,E, most often appear together. As for the rare features, there is also an interesting correlation: while C is the most common feature in the dataset, I is still more like to appear together with H, rather than C. For our problem it means that choice of combination of features may noticable influence the rate of training.
